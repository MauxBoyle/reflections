Q: What happens when you initialize a repository?
Q: Why do you need to do it?

Initializing the repository puts the git metadata in your folder
You need to do it to be able to track changes in git.

Q: How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository?
Q: What value do you think it offers?

It is a good in-between area that allows you to keep your repository organized.

Q: How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?

You can move each changed file to the staging area separately and also
 compare to the previous commit.

Q: What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?

Branches would be helpful when trying something out that might not work, or that might not be implemented in the final version.
